Sales Funnel: From Loops to Sales

Why This Deck Exists

  • Performance is mechanical before it is persuasive
  • Every funnel step answers a single operational question
  • This deck strips folklore, keeps leverage

The Funnel (Law of Averages)

Loops
→ Doors / Leads
→ Visits
→ Contacts
→ Decision Makers (DM)
→ Presentations 
→ Sales

Each step multiplies the next. Break one, everything downstream lies.

STEP 1 — NUMBER OF LOOPS

What Loops Really Measure

Usable, uninterrupted field time ÷ loop cycle time

Loops are constrained by:

  • Day boundaries (start / stop)
  • Interruptions
  • Loop cadence

Step 1 — Core Principles

  1. Protect the day edges
  2. Preload everything the night before
  3. Enforce loop cadence
  4. Kill interruptions fast
  5. Use doors/day as a diagnostic, not a goal

Step 1 — Key Tactics

  • First knock by 11:30 / last knock 6:30
  • Fixed lunch, short breaks
  • 2‑hour loop alarms
  • Disengage after close (<30 min)
  • No off‑loop callbacks or detours
  • Gear, route, fuel ready before the day starts

STEP 2 — DOORS / LEADS

What Doors Really Measure

Route geometry + penetration discipline

This is execution quality, not motivation.

Step 2 — Core Principles

  1. Territory must be worth working
  2. Route geometry beats effort
  3. New doors > reloops
  4. Penetration is rule‑based
  5. Social leakage is the enemy
  6. Presence compounds over time (Jones Effect)

Step 2 — Key Tactics

  • Territory routed before leaders meeting
  • Work tight to the right
  • Logical parking
  • Reloops quarantined (lunch / end of day)
  • 10 attempts before No Answer Final
  • Saturday routing for no‑answers

STEP 3 — VISITS

What Visits Really Are

A derived metric

Visits = Loops × Routed Doors

If visits are low, the problem is upstream.

How to Use Visits

  • Diagnostic only

  • No independent coaching

  • Tripwire metric for:

    • Loop collapse
    • Route skipping
    • Time leakage

STEP 4 — CONTACTS

What Contacts Measure

Probability a door opens to a human

Contacts = Visits × Open Rate

Step 4 — Core Principles

  1. Look legitimate before you knock
  2. Make the knock unmistakable, not aggressive
  3. Stand like you belong there
  4. Exit cleanly, always
  5. Leave the territory better than you found it

Step 4 — High‑Leverage Tactics

  • SEE: Smile, Eye Contact, Enthusiasm
  • Clear, audible knock (once per loop)
  • Stand back, open posture, phone away
  • UPS walk, clean parking
  • 3 NO and GO
  • Brand‑safe exits

STEP 5 — DECISION MAKERS (DMs)

What DMs Measure

Conversation control + authority routing

DMs = Contacts × DM‑Capture Rate

Step 5 — Core Principles

  1. Set the frame before the customer speaks
  2. Compress legitimacy into a short story
  3. Identify the bill‑handler immediately
  4. Do not pitch outside authority
  5. Absorb early objections without stopping
  6. Delay persuasion until the DM is present

Step 5 — Key Tactics

  • Intro → pause → Short Story → why you’re there
  • Exact same script every time
  • “Are you the one who handles that bill?”
  • AIR for early objections
  • Disengage non‑DMs with a smile
  • Never pitch non‑DMs

STEP 6 — PRESENTATIONS

What Presentations Measure

Whether NOW makes NEW inevitable

Presentations = DMs × Presentation‑Start Rate

NOW → NEW Framework

  • NOW = current pain, friction, risk
  • NEW = resolved future state

If NOW is vague, NEW feels optional.

Step 6 — Core Principles

  1. Momentum beats mood (+1 more)
  2. Excavate NOW before offering NEW
  3. Neutralize early objections without stopping
  4. Control the conversation, not the customer
  5. Present everyone — don’t pre‑disqualify
  6. Detach from outcomes, commit to activity

Step 6 — Key Tactics

  • Deeper NOW questions → hot spots
  • Pitch–Talk–Pitch
  • Confident indifference
  • Eliminate doubt words
  • 2 presentations per hour
  • SW³ = WN mindset

STEP 7 — SALES (CLOSING)

What Closing Really Is

Decision architecture, not pressure

Sales = Presentations × Close Rate

Closing Model

  • Anchor to NOW
  • Bridge to NEW
  • Remove friction
  • Ask again

Repeat until resolved.

Step 7 — Core Principles

  1. Anchor every close to customer hotspots
  2. One point at a time (bullet theory)
  3. Re‑enter the close from new angles (DIP)
  4. Answer briefly, then ask again
  5. Make TODAY the natural decision point
  6. Assume normalcy and momentum
  7. Reinforce emotionally after the yes

Step 7 — Key Tactics

  • Scripted close, short & simple
  • Rotating recap order (DIP)
  • TODAY language
  • 2‑option close when appropriate
  • Assume the sale
  • Prepare for verification call

Post‑Close Reinforcement

  • Rehash after order processed
  • Positive exit (SEE, please & thank you)
  • Product confidence
  • Customer name throughout NEW side

Final Truth

  • Mechanics create opportunities
  • Control creates momentum
  • Clarity creates decisions

Fix the funnel in order. Always.
